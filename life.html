Common options

  • Term Life — coverage for 10–30 years to protect income, mortgage, and kids.
  • Whole Life / Final Expense — smaller, permanent coverage for funeral costs and debts.
  • Living benefit riders — some policies include access to funds with certain illnesses (varies by carrier).

Rates depend on age, health, tobacco use, amount, and term. No-exam options may be available.

How much do I need?

  • Many families choose 10–15× annual income (or enough to pay off the mortgage + 5 years of expenses).
  • Pick a term that matches your big goals (kids at home, years left on mortgage, or time to retirement).

Medical bill payment help ("Simplicity" programs)

Not life insurance, but worth knowing: some employers/health plans offer a "Simplicity" service that makes paying medical bills easier.

  • Consolidated billing — one monthly statement for covered services.
  • Interest-free payment plans — spread high out-of-pocket costs over time.
  • Pre-funded payments — the program can pay providers upfront; you repay the program.
  • Rewards & HSA integration — some add credits for future bills and link to your HSA.

Details vary by administrator (e.g., Trustmark, Sanford Health Plan, Humana). These are not major medical insurance.
